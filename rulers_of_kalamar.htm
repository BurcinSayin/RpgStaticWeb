1 IR - 21 IR King Ali, House Inakas

By 1IR King Ali Inakas united the disparate tribes of the Kalamaran peoples and founded the Kingdom of Kalamar. Peaceful relations with the dwarves of the Ka'Asa mountains leads to acquisition of bronze by the Kalamaran people.
21 IR - 43 IR King's Rulakan and Fulakan, House Inakas Frustrated with their father King Ali's peaceful policies, in 21IR prince's Rulakan and Fulakan orchestrated a successful coup and assassinated their father. They then launched military campaigns against their neighbours, conquering all the territory between the Kakidela and Ka'Asa/Byth mountains.
43 IR - 73 IR Queen Lelana, House Inakas Whilst the King's Rulakan and Fulakan were campaigning to build their empire, the realm was actually ruled by Rulakan's wife, Queen Lelana. When Fulakan returned in 43IR he found his brother ill with a mysterious disease. Before he could act Rulakan died and Fulakan disappeared. Queen Lelana officially assumes the mantled of rulership.
73 IR - 89 IR King Kolokar I, House Inakas Kolokar, son of King Rulakan, removes his senile mother and is crowned King of Kalamar in 73IR. During his reign Kolokar's Barrier. Late in his reign the senior patriarchs of house's Eramis and Pilitala seized control of his family and declared themselves in charge. Kolokar was forced to rush back to reclaim  control of his realm and save his family. Whilst on the way back he was thrown from his horse and killed.
89 IR - 159 IR Age of Great Anguish   With the king dead, the patriarchs of both house's Eramis and Pilitala claim the crown for themselves. The land is thrown into a bloody civil war that lasts for 70 years.
159 IR - 171 IR King Thedorus, House Inakas A member of the Royal House of Inakas, Prince Thedorus, with the help of his champion Rovak Fen'doral (a paladin who rode the silver dragon Vevisalakale) reunited the Kingdom of Kalamar and is crowned King.

171 IR - 228 IR Emperor Thedorus, House Inakas After establishing control over the other wayward parts of the his ancestors empire, Thedorus is crowned as the first Emperor of the Kalamaran Empire. At age 89, Thedorus, the most popular emperor in the history of Tellene, died peacefully in his sleep.
228 IR - 236 IR Time of Misfortune (Emperor Vilik I, House Bakar) After Thedorus' son, Lakali, was found brutally murdered the day before his coronation, Vilik, the senior member of House Bakar seized the throne and murdered the rest of House Inakas. A second civil war followed - known as the Time of Misfortune.
236 IR - 254 IR Emperor Vilik I, House Bakar House Bakar emerges triumphant from the Time of Misfortune. The new Emperor secures his realm by dividing it into smaller territories with members of the Royal House of Bakar in the new positions of power.
254 IR - 258 IR Emperor Vilik II, House Bakar With the passing of his father Vilik II assumed the throne of Kalamar. A weak and irresolute man he lasted only 4 years on the throne before being deposed and murdered by his brother. His last act before being overthrown was to issue an edict authorising the establishment of the College of Architecture in Bet Kalamar.
258 IR - 287 IR Emperor Gadadik I, House Bakar After disposing of his elder brother and claiming the Imperial Throne, Emperor Gadadik I, showed the people of Kalamar what it meant to be ruled by a despot. Whilst his father, Vilik I, had been ruthless, Gadadik I was downright sadistic. He ordered executions at the slightest provocation. It was not long before his entire realm began to despise him but the wily Emperor was always one step ahead of his opponents. He died peacefully in his sleep.
287 IR - 299 IR Emperor Vilik III, House Bakar The people of Kalamar rejoiced when Vilik III ascended the Imperial Throne following the death of his father. As a young man Prince Vilik was a handsome, skilled warrior - so unlike his father. For the first few years of his reign he fulfilled the expectations of his people but soon the Emperor grew bored with court life and spent most of his time hunting. His uncle Prince Lakaran, Duke of Salireta, effectively ruled the realm in his stead. Then in 299 IR, the Emperor died heirless on a hunting trip.
299 IR - 335 IR Emperor Lakaran, House Bakar With the death of Emperor Vilik III without an heir, Prince Lakaran Bakar, Duke of Salireta, is crowned Emperor Lakaran of Kalamar - he is already 67 years old when he is crowned. The new Emperor, following in the footsteps of his father, Vilik I, Lakaran is totally ruthless is dealing with his nobility. He establishes raft of new taxes and enriches the treasury, and his favourites, at the expense of his impoverished people. During his 40 year reign he was constantly attempting to suppress rebellions but eventually the pressure grew too great and the legionary commander in Korak, along with the Archduke of P'Bapar, secede from the Empire in 329 IR. The furious Emperor ordered an immediate invasion to retake these regions and punish the rebel leaders but his exhausted armies were unable to accomplish the task. Lakaran had to soothe his anger by ordering the execution of the rebel leader's family members within the Empire, along with the generals who failed to reclaim 'his' lands. Eventually the bitter Emperor died naturally in 335 IR, aged 103. Privately his subjects celebrated his passing.
335 IR - 357 IR Emperor Vilik IV, House Bakar Following his coronation the new Emperor, Vilik IV, decided he needed to reclaim his subjects affection - something he believed (rightly) the Emperor's had lost under the oppressive reign of his father. However, it did not occur to the Emperor (who rarely left the comforts of the palace) that the oppressive taxation the Empire levied was responsible for his people's unrest. No - he decided that they they were restless because they missed the glory days of the Empire, when men like Rulakan and Fulakan ruled - great conquerors. Thus Vilik IV would gain his peoples admiration through mighty conquests and what better target than the realms which rebelled under his fathers rule. Thus in 344 IR the Emperor set off at the head of his legions to retake Korak and P'Bapar. Needless to say Kalamar's long neglected legions were crushed by Koraks finely honed armies and the Emperor narrowly escaped capture. Fleeing back to the Bet Kalamar, the Emperor ordered those legionary commanders who survived the campaign executed and began to distance himself from the whole debacle. Instead, the Emperor consoled himself with pleasures and drinking and soon forgot his need for the peoples affection. He died in 357 IR - possibly as the result of too much drinking.
357 IR - 393 IR Emperor Fonamar, House Bakar Emperor Fonamar succeeded his father to the Imperial Throne in 357 IR. Despite his relatively long reign of 36 years little is known about Emperor Fonamar. It seems as though he was neither despotic nor benevolent. In fact, contemporary sages described the Emperor as something of a simpleton who cared little about the Empire he ruled. He certainly lacked the ruthlessness usually evident in the Bakar family, as demonstrated in 387 IR when Ek'Gekal descended into civil war following the death of the last king without an heir. When told the news of the king's death, Fonamar is reported to have nodded vacantly and then returned to watching the play being staged in his honour. When Ek'Gekal's remaining nobles were expelled and the merchants declared the realm a free state, Kalamar did nothing. Still despite his neglect, many of the Emperors subjects grew to love his - after all he wasn't despotic! Emperor Fonamar died mysteriously in 393 IR.
393 IR - 396 IR Emperor Gadadik II, House Bakar As Emperor Fonamar died without a male heir, his brother Gadadik become emperor upon his death. In Gadadik II the Kalamaran people got the type of Emperor they were used to - cruel and vindictive. Thought to have had a hand in his brothers mysterious death, Gadadik II began his reign his a purge. Not content merely to eliminate those who did question his decisions, Gadadik decided instead to dispose of anybody who might challenge his authority. at first his limited himself to minor nobility but then he turned upon the great houses - even his own family were not safe. Thus three years into his reign, Gadadik's own wife murdered the emperor by pouring acid into his ear - much to the relief of the realm. It is estimated that 6 out of every 10 Kalamaran aristocrats died during Gadadik's brief reign.
396 IR - 399 IR Emperor Malaran I (Regency), House Bakar Following the murder of his father by his mother, the young prince Malaran was crowned Emperor of Kalamar. Since the new Emperor had not reached his majority yet mother, the Empress Dowager ruled as Regent of Kalamar until 399 IR. Contemporaries sages recorded that the Empress Dowager did much to restore stability to the realm and secure the rule of House Bakar, during this it's most dangerous period.
399 IR - 420 IR Emperor Malaran I, House Bakar Emperor Malaran is generally recognised as being the most competent Bakar emperor after Emperor's Vilik I and Kabori. Although the emperor was as ruthless as any of his predecessors, he was also extremely intelligent and understood the value of loyalty. Many of the great houses prospered under his reign and much of the damage done to the Empire under his father was repaired. When the Emperor died in a hunting accident in 420 IR he was genuinely mourned throughout the Empire for the peace and stability his reign brought. as all of the Emperor's children had died young the throne passed to his uncle Galemak upon his death.
420 IR - 435 IR Emperor Galemak I, House Bakar When Emperor Malaran I died without an heir the imperial throne passed to his uncle Galemak I. Galemak's reign was marked by the Fiery Doom. In 421 IR a meteor struck the Alufalik Hills causing widespread devastation in the eastern provinces of the realm. Many of the common people saw this as a sign that gods were angry with the Bakar line and a new wave of rebellions plagued the land, spurred on by the resurgent Guardians of the Hidden Flame. Eventually imperial troops, with the help pro-Bakar priests, restored order and the Guardians melted once more into the shadows. Exploration of the meteor impact site yielded an enormous piece of deep purple amethyst which the realm's most renowned artisans carved into a new throne stylized to represent the lion of House Bakar. Power enchantments are also rumoured to have been woven into the throne. Thereafter, the office of the emperor was colloquially known as the Amethyst Throne.
435 IR - 468 IR Emperor Galemak II, House Bakar Following the death of his father the Amethyst Throne passed to Galemak I's son Galemak II. The new emperor was an extremely insecure man who found the best way he could deal with his insecurities was to surround himself with a vast cohort of flatterers. Thus, during his reign, the size of the imperial court rose from around 900 courtiers in 435 IR to close to 10,000 courtiers by 455 IR. This, combined with the emperors inclination to bestow lavish rewards upon his favourites, meant the realm's need for revenue increased enormously. Thus taxes nearly tripled during the first 20 years of the emperor's reign and more rebellions sprung up throughout the Empire. The most serious occurred in  456 IR when Pekal seceded from the Empire and 459 IR when Paru'Bor was also able to achieved independence. The emperor in Bet Kalamar ordered retaliations but assisted by the elves of Cilorea his forces were repelled. 461 IR saw the elven wizard Shifan appointed the new Imperial Diviner after the death of the archmage Balan Darisek.
468 IR - 505 IR Emperor Galemak III, House Bakar The death of emperor Galemak II meant that his son Galemak III was crowned Emperor of the Kalamaran Empire in 468 IR. Historians generally agree that Galemak III was the weakest and most incompetent emperor to ever sit upon the Amethyst Throne. Whilst many of his predecessors had been cruel and despotic, Galemak III was indecisive when faced with even the most minute decision and so much of the power of the imperial court passed into the hands of the regional aristocracy. This was most effectively demonstrated in 484 IR when the King of Tokis died with no heir. A civil war immediately broke out, as nobles vied for the vacant throne, which the Emperor of Kalamar seemed powerless to prevent. Then in 490 IR the Emperor (or more correctly the Imperial Chancellor, who had by this stage become the de facto ruler of the Empire) ordered an invasion of Tharggy by Dodera and O'Par, Duke Ukadoran II of O'Par ignored the command. When no retaliation came from Bet Kalamar the Duke went one step further and declared the independence of his realm. Thus another realm slipped out of the emperor's grasp.
505 IR - 506 IR Emperor Vilik V, House Bakar Upon the death of Galemak III the imperial throne passed to his brother Vilik V. Mentally unstable before he ascended the throne, the new emperor quickly descended into outright madness. What little government remained in Bet Kalamar quickly collapsed altogether until, after 13 months on the throne the emperor's abdication was orchestrated by powerful figures within the capital, including the Imperial Diviner Shifan according to some sources. The former emperor was taken to the Widow's Tower and never seen again. Many common folk gossip that the emperor is still confined to that terrible tower and that those who pass near the tower can hear the emperors mournful wails of despair.
506 IR - 509 IR Emperor Malaran II, House Bakar With the abdication of Vilik V, his brother Malaran II (the youngest son of Galemak II) was crowned emperor in 506 IR. The new emperor held great promise as he had commanded a legionary army with distinction prior to being crowned. He moved quickly restore central authority in the Empire. However, he died mysteriously in 509 IR. Rumours, at the time, which persist to this day, alleged that members of the Veiled Priesthood were responsible for the deed. If so, many have wondered how they were able to get past Shifan?
509 IR - 544 IR Emperor Gadadik III, House Bakar The death of Malaran without an heir meant the Amethyst Throne now passed to his cousin Gadadik Bakar, Duke of Sobeteta, who was crowned Emperor Gadadik III. An indifferent ruler before being crowned emperor he continued to be so after his coronation. An invasion of Tharggy in 530 IR by Dodera, on the Emperor's orders lead to a disastrous defeat in 532 IR which further weakened imperial authority. In many respects this leads directly to Ek'Kasel's declaration of independence in 538 IR, as King Erasar II of Ek'Kasel was pressured by some within his nobility to break with the Empire. Thus in 544 IR another largely unsuccessful emperor was away.
544 IR -Present Emperor Kabori, House Bakar In 544 IR the fortunes of the Kalamaran Empire changed dramatically when Kabori Bakar was crowned Emperor of the Kalamaran Empire. Many believe Kabori to be the most competent emperor the Bakar family has ever produced and so far he has lived up to that claim. Kabori has reformed the imperial army, turning it into a lethal instrument of imperial will once more, he has considerably strengthened the authority of the Amethyst Throne by bringing his nobles to heel and restoring central authority. He has also resolved the long running Tokisian civil war by installing Adoku I as king. With his authority established and his strength marshalled it seems only a matter of time before the emperor initiates his plans to restore the Kalamaran Empire to its former glory.
