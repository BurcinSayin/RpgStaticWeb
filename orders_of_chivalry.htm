Orders of Chivalry of the Empire of Kalamar

 

The Empire of Kalamar contains three orders of chivalry which are used to denote the precedence of knights within the realm. The order of precedence is dependent upon the age of the knightly order with the oldest order, the Order of the Knights of the Platinum Lion, being considered the highest ranking knighthood and therefore being the most coveted. Several older orders were created by the Inakas line but the knights of these orders were forced to renounce their titles after the ascension of House Bakar to the Imperial Throne or face execution. Additionally, anybody in possession of the regalia of these orders faces immediate arrest and subsequent execution for treason. Hence, these orders have become extinct (although rumours suggest that the Guardians of the Hidden Flame continue to preserve the regalia of these orders in the readiness for the return of the Inakas line).

 

Only the Emperor, or those imperial officials to whom he delegates this authority, may dub a knight a member of one of the Kalamaran orders of chivalry. Thereafter, he or she is granted the title ‘Sir’ of ‘Dame’ and is entitled to be addressed as such. If the recipient already holds a higher title (for example a Baron) then they will generally use the highest title they are entitled to hold.

 

Each of the orders is dividend into four ranks. These are (in ascending order) Knight, Knight Commander, Lord Knight, Grand Knight. With respect to precedence a knight of a higher order ranks before a knight of a lower order if they hold the same rank. Thus, a Knight of the Platinum Lion has precedence over a Knight of the Black Banner. If the knights hold different ranks that the rank determines precedence. Thus, a Knight Commander of the Black Banner has precedence over a Knight of the Platinum Lion. If, however, the holder of either knighthood also holds a noble title than his noble rank determines precedence.

 

For each order there is only one Grand Knight at any point in time and that knight is considered to be the leader of that order, answerable only to the Emperor. This is an exception to the normal feudal rules of precedence in that the Grand Knight of each of these orders ranks higher that a Duke or even a Prince of the Blood. Only Royal Princes and the Emperor rank above them, making these highly coveted positions.

 

Each of the Kalamaran orders is characterised by distinctive regalia unique to that order. This regalia consist of a heavy set chain made of a different metal for each order consisting of coin sized links set with the gemstones of the order at the centre of which hangs a single pendent featuring the arms of the order and a sash with a single pendant featuring the arms of the order that is worn from the right shoulder to the left hip. For Knights the sash is simply the colour of their order. For Knight Commanders, Lord Knights and Grand Knights the sash is bordered by bronze, silver and gold borders respectively. Typically the chain is worn during formal occasions, such as coronations, when attending court etc, whilst the sash is worn during other times.

 

Order of the Knights of the Platinum Lion

 

Founder: Emperor Vilik I, House Bakar

Year of Establishment: 236 IR

Emblem of the Order: Knight mounted on a lion in platinum on a purple field with three platinum crosses

Metal: Gold

Gemstone: Amethyst

                                 Sash colour: Purple

History: Upon finally securing the Imperial Throne after the long civil war known as the Time of Misfortune, Emperor Vilik of the House Bakar needed a method to grant recognition to those who had faithfully served his cause and the old knighthood of the Inakas line would not do since they were reminders of the recently depose regime. He also decided it would be wise to establish a system to align the interests of ambition men within his realm with those of the Imperial Throne. Thus, the Emperor formally established two new knighthoods to replace the ones of old. The premiere knighthood was to be the Order of the Platinum Lion named after the lion on arms of House Bakar (which now became the Kalamaran emblem). Membership of this order was deliberately kept exclusive and was used by the Emperor (and his successors) as a means to ensuring that the restless energies of their subjects were directed towards pleasing the Imperial Throne not coveting it. Most members of this order are members of the imperial family or nobles in their own right. Very few commoners have ever attained this rank throughout the order’s 327 year history. In all only around 5 per cent of the knights in Kalamar belong to this order.

 

Order of the Knights of the Black Banner

 

Founder: Emperor Galemak II, House Bakar

Year of Establishment: 439 IR

Emblem of the Order: Black banner on a silver field

Metal: Silver

Gemstone: Jet

                                 Sash colour: Black

History: Emperor Galemak II was an infamously insecure man who lavished favours upon his courtiers as a means of securing the flattery he so badly craved. Early in his reign the Emperor tried to reward some of his favourites (some of who were rumoured at the time to be his lovers) by creating them Knights of the Platinum Lion. This idea outraged the old elite amongst his court and they strenuously resisted his plans. Fearing the wraith of these powerful nobles but still anxious to reward his favourites the Emperor came up with a compromise plan (or more likely his Imperial Chamberlain came up with the plan and the Emperor claimed credit for it). The Emperor created a new order of knighthood: The Order of the Black Banner. This order was named after the sacred Black Banner which has been unfurled whenever the Emperor (or King) takes the field since the foundation of the Kingdom of Kalamar and is said to have been blessed by the Old Man himself. And since the purpose of this order was to reward his favourites he decreed that knights of the new order shall have precedence over those of the Order of the Vast Empire. Thus, the Emperor was able to indulge in his nepotism without overly alienating the powerful within his realm. Subsequent Emperor’s decided to retain Galemak’s order and now members of the order make up around 25 per cent of the Empire’s chivalry.

 

Order of the Knights of the Vast Empire

 

Founder: Emperor Vilik I, House Bakar

Year of Establishment: 236 IR

Emblem of the Order: Two crossed silver swords on a red field

Metal: Copper

Gemstone: Jacinth

                                 Sash colour: Red

History: At the same time the Order of the Platinum Lion was being created, Emperor Vilik I also signed the decree ordering the creation of a lesser knighthood; the Order of the Vast Empire. This knighthood is easily the most common throughout the empire with most newly created knights being dubbed a member of this order. This order is also the least exclusive of Kalamar’s knightly orders with some famous examples of commoners rising though its ranks, such as Sir Ferasol Mornstar who rose from a gladiator to become the Grand Knight of the Order in 489 IR. It is also knights of this order who make up much of the Empire’s mounted chivalry in the legions. In all, about 70 per cent of the knights in Kalamar belong to this order.
